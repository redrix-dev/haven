Haven, the community chat app.
Sentence case. Comma, then a flat noun phrase — no "next-gen", no "platform", no verbs pretending to be features. The product is a chat app for communities. State it, period.
Do
"Haven, the community chat app."
Plain, comma-paced, ends on the thing it actually is.
Don't
"Welcome to Haven — the next-gen, AI-powered community platform!"
Hype words, em-dash drama, exclamation. Three lies in one sentence.
Do
"Your community deserves better."
Honest comparative. Implies the alternative without naming it.
Don't
"🚀 Supercharge your community with Haven 💪"
Emoji as decoration, Silicon-Valley verbs. Haven prefers quiet confidence.
Do
"No roadmap theater. Here's what's actually shipped."
First-person voice, dry. Calls out the genre, then delivers.
Don't
"We're so excited to announce our exciting new release!"
Press-release tone. Excitement told, not shown. Two "excite"s.
